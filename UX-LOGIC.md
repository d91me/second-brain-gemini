# UX Logic: Agent Second Brain + Gemini CLI

This document defines the interaction model for using your Second Brain across mobile (Telegram) and desktop (Terminal), and how the Gemini CLI extension handles the "Brain" logic.

## 1. The Multi-Surface Workflow

### Surface A: Mobile Capture (Telegram Bot)
- **Primary Use**: Quick capture of thoughts, voice notes, photos, and Todoist tasks while on the go.
- **Mechanism**: The Telegram bot (running as a background service) acts as the "Inbox". It writes everything directly into your `vault/daily/` files.
- **Feedback**: The bot sends you a daily HTML summary report at 21:00, generated by calling the Gemini CLI extension.

### Surface B: Desktop Research (Terminal / Gemini CLI)
- **Primary Use**: Deep retrieval, brainstorming, and vault maintenance.
- **Mechanism**: You open `gemini` in your terminal. Because the extension is linked, you have "Superpowers" over your vault.
- **Example**: `gemini "What were those 3 marketing ideas I mentioned in voice notes last week?"`
- **Result**: Gemini uses the `/agent-memory` and `/graph-builder` skills to scan the vault and provide a synthesized answer.

## 2. Integrated "Telegram-to-CLI" Logic

### Can the extension be the bot?
Yes. The extension is the **Logic Engine**, while the bot is the **Interface**.
- The `agent-second-brain` extension provides the instructional context (`GEMINI.md`) that tells Gemini *how* to process your life.
- The `src/d_brain/bot` script is a lightweight "listener" that feeds data into the engine.

### Authorization Handshake (OAuth Logic)
To authorize your Gemini CLI directly from the Telegram bot:

1.  **Initiate**: You send `/auth` to your Telegram bot.
2.  **Challenge**: The bot generates a secure session token or opens an OAuth flow for your Google Account.
3.  **Approval**: Once you approve on your phone, the bot executes a headless command on your Docker server:
    ```bash
    gemini auth set-token <captured_token>
    ```
4.  **Verification**: The bot replies in Telegram: "✅ Terminal Authorized. You can now use `gemini` commands on the server."

## 3. User Experience Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The "Voice-to-Task" Loop
1.  **User (Telegram)**: Records voice note: "Remind me to buy milk and email the contract to John tomorrow."
2.  **Bot**: Transcribes via Deepgram → saves to `vault/daily/2026-03-15.md`.
3.  **Gemini (Extension)**: Triggered via cron or `/process` button.
4.  **Gemini**: Executes `/todoist-ai add-task "Buy milk"`, `/todoist-ai add-task "Email contract to John" due:tomorrow`.
5.  **Bot (Telegram)**: "Tasks created! 🥛 Buy milk | ✉️ Email John."

### Scenario 2: The "Memory Recall" Session
1.  **User (Terminal)**: `gemini`
2.  **User**: `/agent-memory search "Project X architecture"`
3.  **Extension**: Scans `core` and `active` tiers of the vault.
4.  **Gemini**: "Found it. You decided on a microservices approach on Feb 12th because of scaling concerns. Link: [[projects/project-x/architecture]]"

### Scenario 3: Automated Health
1.  **System (Sunday 00:00)**: Cron runs `gemini "/vault-health fix-links"`.
2.  **Extension**: Finds broken wiki-links, repairs them, and updates your Maps of Content (MOC).
3.  **Bot (Telegram)**: "Good morning! I've repaired 12 links and updated your Business MOC. Your Vault Health is now 94/100."

---

## 4. Why this is better than a standalone bot
By moving the logic into a **Gemini CLI Extension**:
- **Logic remains local**: Your processing rules and prompts are in your repo, not hardcoded in a remote API.
- **Unified Intelligence**: The same "Brain" that processes your Telegram notes is the one you chat with in your IDE or Terminal.
- **Extensibility**: You can easily add new skills (like `/seo` or `/blog`) that now have access to your personal goals and contacts.
